Our Iranian Favourite Food

Zereshk Polo is one of the most famous and traditional dishes in Iranian cuisine. It is made with fragrant saffron rice, barberries, and usually served with chicken. The sweet and sour flavor of the barberries, combined with the aroma of saffron, creates a unique and delicious taste. Its colorful appearance and rich flavor make it a popular choice for family gatherings, celebrations, and special occasions. Zereshk Polo is not only a beloved meal in Iran but also a symbol of the country’s rich culinary heritage.

Sample Zereshk Polo Recipe

  • Basmati Rice 4 cups
  • Chicken pieces 4 pieces (legs or breasts)
  • Onions 2 large onions
  • Barberries (Zereshk) 1 cup
  • Brewed Saffron As much as your heart desires (be generous!)
  • Tomato paste 2 tablespoons
  • Butter 50 grams
  • Slivered pistachios and almonds For garnish (optional)
  • Fresh lime juice 1 tablespoon
  • Salt, black pepper, and turmeric

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for at least 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. In a large pot, bring water to a boil and add a pinch of salt. Add the soaked rice and cook until it is al dente (about 6-7 minutes). Drain the rice and set aside.
  3. In a separate pan, heat some oil and sauté the onions until they are golden brown. Remove half of the onions for garnish.
  4. Add the chicken pieces to the pan with the remaining onions. Season with salt, black pepper, and turmeric. Cook until the chicken is browned on all sides.
  5. Add tomato paste to the chicken and stir well. Pour in enough water to cover the chicken, bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until the chicken is cooked through (about 30-40 minutes).
  6. In another pan, melt butter and sauté the barberries for a few minutes until they are plump. Be careful not to burn them.
  7. To assemble the dish, layer the cooked rice with the chicken mixture in a large serving dish. Drizzle brewed saffron over the top and sprinkle with sautéed barberries and reserved fried onions. Garnish with slivered pistachios and almonds if desired.
  8. Serve hot with fresh lime juice on the side for an extra burst of flavor.
